The Black Sphinx


Beneath the glaring desert sun,

surrounded by a sea of sands,

a form not built by human hands;

monolith of flawless ebon.


A gaze devoid of life or light,

unmoving, yet all-seeing.

To know the secret that it holds,

would you offer up your soul?


CHORUS:

With lion body, and head of man;

there it stands amidst the sands.

Softly spoken: promise of power.

The Black Sphinx calls~

(Dare you answer?)


To solve it's riddle all are driven,

(to find the secrets it has hidden.)

It matters not by whom it's sent,

(nor by what malefic intent.)


To claim its power for their own,

(the seeds of war, now are sewn.)

For king and country we will go,

(When they speak, we make it so.)

blinded to its true intention.
